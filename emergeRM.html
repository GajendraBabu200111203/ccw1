BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY

Blockchain is a shared, immutable ledger for recording transactions, tracking assets and building trust. Blockchain is ideal for delivering that information because it provides immediate, shared and completely transparent information stored on an immutable ledger that can be accessed only by permissioned network members. A blockchain network can track orders, payments, accounts, production and much more. And because members share a single view of the truth, you can see all details of a transaction end to end, giving you greater confidence, as well as new efficiencies and opportunities.

MACHINE LEARNING

Machine learning (ML) is a field of inquiry devoted to understanding and building methods that 'learn', that is, methods that leverage data to improve performance on some set of tasks.[1] It is seen as a part of artificial intelligence. Machine learning algorithms build a model based on sample data, known as training data, in order to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed to do so.[2] Machine learning algorithms are used in a wide variety of applications, such as in medicine, email filtering, speech recognition, agriculture, and computer vision, where it is difficult or unfeasible to develop conventional algorithms to perform the needed tasks.[3][4] A subset of machine learning is closely related to computational statistics, which focuses on making predictions using computers, but not all machine learning is statistical learning.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Artificial intelligence (AI) is intelligence—perceiving, synthesizing, and infering information—demonstrated by machines, as opposed to intelligence displayed by animals and humans. Example tasks in which this is done include speech recognition, computer vision, translation between (natural) languages, as well as other mappings of inputs.

DATA SCIENTIST

As a Data Scientist, you will be dealing with unstructured data and will be responsible for gathering and analyzing it to create more sophisticated and structured data. Do you like math and statistics? If yes, then this domain is for you as you will utilize the interdisciplinary knowledge of computer science, math and statistics. The trends that you identify will lead to business innovations and significant business breakthroughs. Programing languages such as Python, SQL and SAS and Data Visualization tools such as Tableau, PowerBI and Excel are essential in this field.

ROBOTIS ENGINEER

Do you often find yourself disassembling electronics and assembling them to understand how stuff works? If yes, then you can build a successful career in Robotics Engineering. The careers in his space will require expert knowledge of electronics, programming, computer science, cognitive psychology and mechanics. With expert knowledge, you will design the robots and even the machines that build the robots. Interesting, isn’t it?

PRODUCT MANAGER

Product management involves analysing the customer demographics and customer behaviour to develop appropriate products and services. Product management careers will deal with strategies to launch a new product line, expand existing products and services, and more. Knowledge of advertising, strategic management, marketing, promotion and more is essential to be a successful Product Manager.
